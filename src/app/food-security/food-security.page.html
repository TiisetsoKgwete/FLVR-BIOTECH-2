Global Challenge

The system is failing across four linked fronts.

Understanding the interconnected crises threatening global food security.

Food Shortage

Climate shocks, yield instability, and constrained land expansion are widening the gap between demand and supply.

Food Wastage

Large portions of global production are lost after harvest or wasted after distribution, even while hunger persists.

Agricultural Pollution

High-emission production models intensify energy use, water stress, and climate-linked instability across supply chains.

Animal-System Pressure

Industrial livestock systems magnify welfare concerns, resource inefficiency, and antibiotic dependency at scale.

Framework

Six Pillars of Food Security

Availability

Sufficient quantities of food consistently available

Access

Ability to obtain appropriate and nutritious food

Utilization

Proper biological use of food through diet and health

Stability

Consistent availability and access over time

Agency

Ability to make decisions about food consumption

Sustainability

Long-term environmental and economic viability
